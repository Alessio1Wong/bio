Investigations of Factors Influencing Air-Bubble Drag Reduction on Ship Hulls

In this award-winning research, I investigated the effects of various factors on air-bubble drag reduction for ship hulls. By constructing a physical model and conducting experiments, I analysed parameters including relative velocity, hull shape, and vertical air-injection configuration. The data was processed using MATLAB to derive quantitative and qualitative relationships that inform future designs for energy-efficient naval technology.

This project won the First Prize in the Shanghai Youth Science and Technology Innovation Competition and highlighted the potential for drag-reduction technology to advance eco-friendly ship designs and applications.
